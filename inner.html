Cockroaches

Cockroaches travel through filthy environments and pick up disease-causing organisms on their bodies that can be mechanically spread to humans and their food. In addition, cockroaches feces and the exoskeletons they shed are allergens that can exacerbate human allergies or breathing conditions.

Rodents

Rodents are responsible for many diseases affecting public health. Infected rodents transmit salmonella to humans by contaminating food with their urine and feces. Rodents and their fleas are also carriers of diseases such as, flea borne-Murine Typhus.

Fleas

Fleas spread diseases to humans and pests after biting and contracting diseases from infected rodents. The cat flea (Centocephalides felis) is the most common flea and comprises nearly 90 percent of the fleas found on dogs and cats.

Ticks

Ticks are blood-sucking parasites that spread disease to people and pets through bites. Some species of ticks are carriers of diseases such as, Lyme disease. Lyme disease is caused by a bacteria which ticks contract from infected rodents.

Bed bugs

In recent years, public health agencies across the country have been overwhelmed by complaints about bed bugs (Cimex lectutarius). Bed bugs can be found in areas where people sleep of spend a significant period of time. Bed bugs are masters of hiding. Their small flat bodies allow them to fit into the smallest spaces and remain in place for long period of time, even without a blood meal.
